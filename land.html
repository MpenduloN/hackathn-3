About Land

The purpose of the National Action Programme (NAP) for South Africa is to identify factors contributing to desertification, land degradation and drought as well as to implement practical measures necessary to combat desertification and to mitigate the effects of drought.

Land degradation is a global problem that affects the livelihoods of millions of people, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions. It is caused by a combination of natural and human factors, including deforestation, overgrazing, unsustainable agricultural practices, and climate change.

Desertification is the process by which fertile land becomes desert, typically as a result of drought, deforestation, or inappropriate agriculture. It is a significant environmental issue that can lead to reduced agricultural productivity, food insecurity, and loss of biodiversity.

Drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall, leading to a shortage of water. It can have severe impacts on agriculture, water supply, and ecosystems, and can exacerbate the effects of land degradation and desertification.

Combating desertification and land degradation requires a multi-faceted approach, including sustainable land management practices, reforestation, soil conservation, and water management. It also involves raising awareness and educating communities about the importance of protecting and restoring land resources.
